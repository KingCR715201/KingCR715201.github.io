What are Greenhouse Gases?

Greenhouse gases include carbon dioxide (CO2), methane (CH4), nitrous oxide (N2O), and fluorinated gases. These gases absorb infrared radiation and trap heat in our atmosphere.

  • CO2: Emitted from fossil fuel burning, deforestation, and industrial processes.
  • CH4: Released from landfills, livestock, and natural gas production.
  • N2O: Comes from agricultural activities and industrial practices.
